= Architecture Decision Record: 0002-adopt-envelope-first-design-discipline

# 2. Adopt the Envelope-First Design Discipline

Date: 2026-06-21

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Panoply is extracted from the lessons of AffineScript, a broad experimental
language exploring affine types, borrowing, effects, rows, traits, faces, and
multiple backends. AffineScript's breadth is deliberate, but breadth created a
recurring trust problem: a large surface invites a single, global "is it safe?"
question that no whole-language proof can honestly answer for every program,
feature combination, and backend at once.

The repository needs a single, normative statement of what Panoply *is* before
any of its mechanisms — Core checker, evidence formats, manifest schema,
backend contracts — are built. Without that charter, the mechanisms would
accrete without a shared rule, and "safety" would drift back into an unscoped
global claim. At the time of this decision the repository is a freshly
scaffolded RSR template carrying none of Panoply's identity, so the charter is
the first load-bearing artefact to establish.

## Decision

Adopt the *envelope-first design discipline* as Panoply's founding principle,
recorded canonically in
xref:../architecture/DESIGN-DISCIPLINE.adoc[docs/architecture/DESIGN-DISCIPLINE.adoc].

The discipline fixes the following non-negotiables:

- Panoply is defined by three artefacts — a checked Core, explicit evidence,
  and a safety-envelope manifest — not by a surface language, a family of
  faces, or a global safety claim.
- The central rule governs every layer: **no guarantee without an envelope; no
  envelope without evidence; no composition without a composition rule.**
- Safety is scoped and parameterised by feature subset, projection, compiler
  mode, backend contract, and proof/check status — never asserted globally.
- Surface syntaxes are *projections* (which must elaborate accepted programs
  into Core + evidence + manifest), not *faces*; apparent equivalence between
  projections must be witnessed, not assumed.
- Backends define their own contracts and do not inherit source guarantees;
  a program receives only the guarantees a backend's envelope names and its
  checks, proofs, or runtime discipline support.
- Experimental features are permitted but may not borrow stronger claims from
  neighbours; unsupported combinations must be rejected, downgraded, or
  explicitly marked outside-envelope.

The charter is normative. Future mechanisms must trace back to its clauses, and
any divergence is reconciled by amending the charter through a subsequent ADR
rather than by weakening behaviour in code.

## Consequences

What becomes easier or more difficult to do because of this change?

### Positive

- Every safety claim Panoply makes becomes explicit, scoped, and inspectable
  via a per-program manifest, instead of resting on an unprovable global
  assertion.
- New surface syntaxes and new backends can be added incrementally: each is
  accountable to the same projection/contract obligations without requiring a
  whole-language re-proof.
- Experimental work is unblocked without contaminating proved features, because
  unsupported combinations are forced to declare their weakened status.
- Agents and contributors have one normative source of truth for "what is
  Panoply," reducing conflation with AffineScript.

### Negative

- More upfront ceremony: a program is not "done" when it typechecks; it must
  also project, carry evidence, and emit a manifest.
- The mechanisms named by the charter (Core checker, evidence formats, manifest
  schema, backend contracts, projection-equivalence witnesses) are not yet
  implemented, so the charter currently leads the code it governs.
- Projection equivalence and cross-backend reasoning require explicit witnesses
  and composition rules, which is more work than assuming sameness.

### Neutral

- Panoply remains a narrowing of one AffineScript lesson, not a rejection of
  it; AffineScript continues to explore breadth independently.
- The charter is amendable: this ADR can be superseded by a later ADR as the
  discipline is refined against real implementation experience.
