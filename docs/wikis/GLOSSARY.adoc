= Panoply Glossary
:toc: preamble
:icons: font

The vocabulary of the envelope-first discipline. Definitions are normative to
the xref:../architecture/DESIGN-DISCIPLINE.adoc[charter]; this page expands and
cross-links them.

Core::
  The single *checked* target into which every accepted program projects. All
  guarantees are reasoned about in Core, never in a surface syntax. (`src/core/`)

Projection::
  A surface syntax *together with its obligation* to elaborate every accepted
  program into Core + evidence + manifest. Replaces the idea of a "face": a
  projection is accountable. (`src/projections/`)

Face::
  *Rejected term.* A surface trusted merely because it looks like the language.
  Panoply has projections, not faces — appearance earns nothing.

Evidence::
  The *witness* behind a claim, of one of three kinds: a *proof* (mechanical),
  a *check* (decision-procedure result), or a *runtime discipline* (declared,
  enforced invariant). No envelope without evidence. (`src/evidence/`)

Envelope::
  A *named, scoped guarantee*. States *which* guarantee holds and under *what
  parameters* — feature subset, projection, compiler mode, backend contract,
  proof/check status.

Manifest::
  The *per-program, per-backend* artefact listing the envelopes a program earned
  *and*, explicitly, the guarantees it did *not* earn. The deliverable — not the
  absence of type errors. (`src/manifest/`)

Backend contract::
  A backend's own statement of which guarantees it can uphold, by what means
  (check / proof / runtime discipline), and which it cannot. Guarantees are
  *backend-relative*; nothing survives lowering for free. (`src/backends/`)

Composition rule::
  A *named* rule under which two enveloped components combine into a larger
  guarantee. Absent such a rule, no combined guarantee is asserted.

Scope parameters::
  The five axes that parameterise every envelope: *feature subset*, *projection*,
  *compiler mode*, *backend contract*, and *proof/check status*. Safety is scoped
  along these, never asserted globally.

Outside-envelope::
  A disposition for an accepted-but-unsupported feature combination: it is
  flagged as carrying *no* Panoply guarantee (as opposed to being *rejected* or
  *downgraded*).

Downgrade::
  A disposition for an unsupported combination: it is accepted, but the manifest
  reflects the *weaker* guarantee actually earned.

The central rule::
  _No guarantee without an envelope. No envelope without evidence. No composition
  without a composition rule._

AffineScript::
  A *separate* project — a broad experimental language (affine types, borrowing,
  effects, rows, traits, faces, multiple backends). Panoply is an envelope-first
  *discipline* extracted from one of its lessons; the two share no
  implementation. Do not conflate them.
